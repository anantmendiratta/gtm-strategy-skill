# Channel Playbooks by Motion

Channels are not interchangeable. A channel that carries trust in DTC apparel does not carry trust in legal SaaS. Use this reference to match channels to the chosen growth motion.

**The core filter for every channel: does trust travel through this channel for THIS category?**

---

## Quick Reference: Channels by Motion

| Motion | Primary channels | Supporting | Defer / avoid in Year 1 |
|--------|------------------|------------|-------------------------|
| Integration-Led | Host platform marketplace (Slack, VS Code, Salesforce AppExchange, Notion, Chrome), host developer community, co-marketing with host | Story-Led content for awareness, PLG within the integration | Standalone paid acquisition, events not tied to host platform ecosystem |
| Product-Led | In-product virality, freemium → paid triggers, template/integration SEO | Founder brand, community | Paid sales team, ABM, expensive PR |
| Sales-Led | Outbound SDR, ABM, industry events, analyst relations | Founder LinkedIn, category content, strategic partnerships | Broad paid social, Product Hunt, general press |
| Story-Led | Founder personal brand, long-form content, podcast tours, community seeding | One PR moment per quarter, curated events | Paid social (before category exists), broad ads |
| Ads-Led | Meta, Google, YouTube, TikTok, CTV (whichever math works) | Creative testing pipeline, landing page optimization | Founder brand (helps but not primary), events |
| Creative-Led | Organic social (IG/TikTok/YouTube), OOH, PR around campaigns, collabs | Paid social behind strongest creative, retail/packaging | Cold outbound, performance-only testing |
| Model-Led | PR around the model, comparison content, demo-the-advantage trials | Founder brand explaining the shift | Generic SEO, undifferentiated paid ads |
| Channel-Led | The ONE dominant channel | Whatever cross-channel halo emerges | Everything not reinforcing the dominant channel |
| Data-Led | Whatever gets usage fastest (often PLG or free tier) | Data-advantage content, partnerships | Premium paid (until flywheel proven) |
| Event/Community-Led | Curated dinners, industry events, own conference, community building | Founder brand, podcast appearances | Broad paid, cold outbound, performance channels |

## Trust-by-Category Matrix

Regardless of motion, some channels carry trust in some categories and not others. Use this to filter.

| Category | Channels that carry trust | Channels that don't (early) |
|----------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| **B2B SaaS (professional services)** | LinkedIn founder content, warm intros, industry associations, case studies, curated dinners | Twitter/X (weak), Instagram, Product Hunt (wrong buyer), cold outbound |
| **Fintech / Investment tools** | Founder brand, niche investor forums (fintwit, ValuePickr, WSB for consumer), word of mouth | Mass media (too slow), paid social (trust deficit), app store ratings |
| **Healthcare / Medtech** | Peer referrals, medical association networks, conferences (HIMSS, ACC, ASCO), clinical evidence | Consumer social, Product Hunt, general press |
| **Dev tools / Developer-adjacent** | Hacker News, GitHub, dev.to, Twitter/X, YouTube demos, Product Hunt, conferences | LinkedIn (too slow), traditional PR |
| **AI tools (LLM-powered, AI-native)** | AI tool directories (Futurepedia, There's An AI For That), LLM marketplaces (Claude/OpenAI/Anthropic app stores), integration ecosystems (VS Code, Slack, Notion, Salesforce marketplaces), benchmark/eval publishing, founder AI content, HuggingFace for ML-adjacent | Generic paid social, mass press (wrong audience), cold outbound without personalization |
| **No-code / Operator tools (business buyers)** | LinkedIn, operator communities (Indie Hackers, ON Deck, specific Slacks), founder voice, direct outreach | Hacker News (wrong audience), Product Hunt (wrong buyer) |
| **Consumer apps (habit-forming)** | Word of mouth, community seeding, influencer seeding (organic), referral mechanics, TikTok if Gen-Z/Millennial | PR (too ephemeral), paid UA (before retention proven), LinkedIn |
| **Legal / Compliance tools** | Bar association networks, legal LinkedIn communities, founder credibility content, warm intros from trusted lawyers | Cold outbound, consumer channels, general press |
| **Education / Upskilling** | Community seeding, peer referrals, LinkedIn thought leadership, cohort-based networks | Paid ads (until retention proven), mass media |
| **DTC consumer (apparel, beauty, wellness)** | Instagram, TikTok, creator/influencer seeding, retail presence, PR around distinctive campaigns | LinkedIn, B2B forums |
| **DTC consumer (home, pet, parenting)** | Instagram, YouTube, podcast ads, affiliate, PR | Twitter/X, Product Hunt |
| **Hardware / consumer electronics** | YouTube (reviewer-seeded), tech press, Kickstarter/Indiegogo for early traction, retail placement | Pure social ads early (friction too high) |
| **Marketplace (two-sided)** | Depends on which side is harder to acquire — focus channels there first; often community, founder brand, and referral | Generic paid on both sides (expensive, poor retention) |
| **Regulated B2B (legal, compliance, security, healthcare IT)** | Curated events, industry associations, warm intros, analyst relations, founder credibility content | Paid social, broad outbound, consumer channels |

## Default Channel Stack for Most Early-Stage (Story-Led + Sales-Led + Trust-Heavy)

Apply in this order. Justify any deviation with the trust question.

### 1. Founder Personal Brand (always first in trust-heavy)
The founder's credibility IS the product's credibility until PMF.

- **Where:** Platform where the ICP actually is (LinkedIn for B2B/operators, Twitter/X for tech/investors/developers, specific community forums for niche)
- **What:** Content anchored to the 3 constants. Not product updates — insights, proof points, perspectives that demonstrate the same intelligence the product provides
- **Cadence:** Minimum 3×/week. Consistency > perfection.
- **Rule:** Every post useful whether or not the reader buys.

### 2. Engineered Word of Mouth
Design sharing into the product itself.

- Shareable output cards / reports / insights with the user's handle
- Referral mechanics tied to the product's core value (not generic "get a discount")
- Make it easy for users to look smart by sharing what the product surfaces

### 3. Community Seeding
Identify 2–3 communities where your ICP already gathers. Show up with value, not ads.

- Never lead with the product. Lead with the insight the product enables.
- Spend 30 days contributing before mentioning you have a product.
- Get the 5 most respected voices in each community as users before anyone else.

### 4. SEO (only after PMF signals)
Invest here only after you know what searches your best users come from.

- Map content to questions your retained users actually had before they found you
- Long-tail, high-intent beats high-volume generic in Year 1
- Don't hire an SEO agency before Month 6

### 5. One PR Moment Per Quarter
Not a press release strategy. One meaningful story per quarter that advances the category narrative.

- The story is always a category story, not a product story
- You are the proof point inside someone else's story
- Target publications your ICP actually reads — not TechCrunch if your users are lawyers

## Default Channel Stack for PLG / Consumer

### 1. In-Product Virality / Activation
The product must generate its own acquisition mechanism.

- Collaboration invites (Figma, Slack, Loom)
- Shareable output with product branding (Cal.com links, Loom video cards, Calendly invites)
- Referral mechanics tied to product value

### 2. Freemium with Clear Upgrade Trigger
A free tier must exist and must be generous enough to create habit.

- Upgrade trigger should be hit by 30–50% of activated users within 30 days
- Paid features should feel earned, not gated arbitrarily

### 3. Template / Integration / Use-Case SEO
PLG winners SEO on what their users build.

- Notion templates. Canva templates. Zapier integrations. Airtable use-cases.
- Each piece of content is a landing page for a specific user intent

### 4. Community (Templates, Gallery, Forum)
Users showing off product output = marketing.

- Dedicated subreddit, Discord, or in-product gallery
- Surface the top users as the face of the product

### 5. Founder Brand (supporting)
Even in PLG, founder brand amplifies everything else.

## Default Channel Stack for Ads-Led / Consumer DTC

### 1. Creative Engine
Ship 10–30 new creatives per week from day 1. The winning creative is unknown; you must test to find it.

- Static → video → UGC → testimonial → founder story → before/after → listicle
- Each creative tested against the same audience; fastest feedback loop wins

### 2. Meta + Google (the workhorses)
For most consumer DTC, 60–80% of paid spend goes to Meta + Google in Year 1.

- Meta for discovery / interrupt intent
- Google for harvest / category intent

### 3. Post-Purchase Retention Engine
Ads-Led without retention is a Ponzi. Email, SMS, loyalty, subscription all run in parallel to paid.

### 4. Creator / Influencer (supporting)
- Paid partnerships with micro-influencers in the exact ICP niche
- Own creator program with performance-based compensation

### 5. One PR Moment Per Quarter or a Distinctive Brand Campaign
Paid amplifies something; that something comes from creative or PR.

## Channels to Defer Until Retention is Proven

These channels amplify what's working; they don't create what isn't.

| Channel | Activate when | Risk of early activation |
|---------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Paid social | Day-90 retention > 60% (for consumer) OR LTV model validated | Burns budget filling a leaky bucket |
| Influencer partnerships | You have clear, specific proof points | Inauthentic, damages trust in trust-first categories |
| Sales team / SDRs | Founder has closed 10+ of the ideal deal shape personally | Expensive, masks PMF signals |
| Partnership / BD | Product can stand alone | Distracts founders; rarely moves fast in Year 1 |
| Conferences / events | You have case studies and community | Expensive, low conversion without social proof |
| Analyst relations | ACV > $50K AND you have 5+ named-customer references | Slow, expensive, not actionable early |

## Special Cases

### ICP doesn't have an obvious online community
(Senior healthcare professionals, operational owners in specific geographies, some regulated industries.)

- Go offline first: industry associations, local groups, specific professional networks
- LinkedIn DMs with a genuine insight (not a pitch) as the opening move
- Find the 3 people in the space everyone respects — make them believers before anything else

### Founder is not comfortable with personal brand content
This is a Year 1 constraint to work through, not around.

Options in order:
1. Start with written content (easier than video for most)
2. Start with reposts + commentary on others' content (lower stakes)
3. Co-create content with an early power user whose credibility you borrow
4. If truly impossible, the most credible co-founder or operator leads content

**Do not substitute a brand account for founder voice in Year 1 in trust-heavy categories.** Brand accounts without founder trust are marketing. Founder accounts are trust.

### Channel was working, now isn't
Channels drift. Algorithms change. CPCs creep. Creative fatigues. When a channel that was the engine is now the drag:

1. Diagnose first — is it creative fatigue, targeting decay, audience saturation, or algorithmic shift?
2. Test adjacent channels before panicking (IG → TikTok, Google Search → Google Shopping)
3. If the channel is truly exhausted, the next channel often requires redesigning the product or the offer — not just buying ads somewhere new

### Multi-sided marketplaces
Focus acquisition on the harder-to-acquire side first. Usually supply before demand (Uber started by recruiting drivers; Airbnb started by photographing hosts' listings).
