# Worked Example: Clartha GTM Strategy

Clartha is an AI-native investment intelligence product for serious retail investors in India.
Use this as a quality and format reference — the specificity, tone, and structure here is the bar to match.

---

## Part I — The Constants

These are the three things serious investors have always wanted and will always want. They do not change with markets, technology cycles, or regulation.

### 1 — Signal Over Noise

The serious investor's core problem is not lack of data. It is drowning in it. CNBC, Telegram groups, analyst reports, Twitter — everything feels urgent, nothing is actionable. Clartha's job is to surface the right signal, at the right moment, for each investor's specific portfolio and thesis.

### 2 — Faster Conviction

The distance between 'interesting stock' and 'I have a view' is expensive — in time, in missed opportunities, in cognitive load. Collapsing that distance from days to minutes is permanently valuable regardless of what the market is doing.

### 3 — Fewer Costly Mistakes

Losses hurt more than equivalent gains feel good. This is not psychology — it is wiring. Any tool that measurably reduces bad decisions has permanent demand. This is Clartha's deepest promise: not just finding the right ideas, but avoiding the wrong ones.

These three constants are Clartha's North Star. When the team debates features, partnerships, or priorities — ask one question: does this serve signal over noise, faster conviction, or fewer mistakes? If the answer is no, deprioritise.

## Part III — GTM Strategy

Clartha's GTM is not a growth strategy. It is a trust-engineering strategy. In financial products, trust is the moat — and it must be earned before it can be scaled.

### The Core Principle: Create a Category

Do not market a product. Create a category. The category is: AI-native investment intelligence for serious retail investors. Everything Zerodha, Tickertape, Smallcase, and Screener do is fragmented tools. Clartha is the system that connects them. Own that framing loudly and consistently.

### The Trust Ladder

| Stage | What You Are Proving | To Whom |
|-------|---------------------|---------|
| 0 → 1 | The product actually works | Yourselves |
| 1 → 500 | It works for real investors | Early community |
| 500 → 5,000 | It is worth paying for | The market |
| 5,000 → 50,000 | It spreads on its own | Growth engine |

Year 1 covers stages 1 through 3. Stage 4 is a Year 2 problem. Do not confuse distribution with traction.

### Positioning Statement

> *Clartha gives serious retail investors the same depth of intelligence that institutional research teams have — without the institutional infrastructure. One system for signal over noise, faster conviction, and fewer costly mistakes.*

### Content Engine — What to Publish

All content is anchored to the three constants. Founders publish — not a brand account. Trust in finance is personal first, brand second.

- **Weekly:** 'Here is what changed in [sector] this week that most investors missed' — tied to Clartha's signal detection
- **Weekly:** 'The 3 things in [company]'s annual report no one is talking about' — demonstrates document intelligence
- **Bi-weekly:** 'How this macro shift maps to your portfolio' — portfolio-aware thinking made visible
- **Monthly:** One deep-dive thesis built using Clartha, shared in full — the product is the marketing

### Pricing Architecture

| Tier | Price | Target User | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------------|--------------|
| Starter | Free | Curious investors | Market pulse, basic screener, 5 AI queries/day |
| Pro | Rs. 1,499/month | Serious self-directed investors | Full signals, deep document AI, portfolio linking, unlimited queries |
| Power | Rs. 3,499/month | Semi-pros & advisors | Multi-portfolio, API access, early features, priority support |

Annual pricing at 20% discount. Do not launch with a discount. Launch at full price with exclusivity — invite-only, waitlist, founder-gated access. This filters for serious users, anchors price expectations early, and signals category positioning.

## Part IV — Year 1 Execution Plan

### Q1 — Prove It Works
*Months 1–3 | Objective: Internal validation + Closed 100*

**What To Do**
- Run Clartha on 30 real companies. Find 3–5 cases where the signal engine surfaces something material before it becomes news. Document them with screenshots — this is your entire early marketing.
- Identify your 10 founder-users from personal networks. Serious investors who will be brutal, not nice. Onboard them personally.
- Hand-pick 100 closed beta users from fintwit, Telegram investing groups, ValuePickr, and Zerodha Varsity alumni. Full Pro access free. Weekly 20-minute feedback calls.
- Track one metric only: do they return the next day? Daily return rate is your product-market fit signal in this phase.
- Build the proof file: 5 specific, documented cases of Clartha surfacing signal early. These become testimonials, content, and investor material.

**Success Criteria — End of Q1**

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Closed beta users | 100 |
| Day-7 return rate (beta) | > 60% |
| Documented proof points | 5 specific cases |
| Founder-user calls completed | 10 |

**Investor Update — Q1 — Months 1–3**

> *We have completed our closed beta with 100 hand-picked serious retail investors. Day-7 retention is running at 68%. We have documented 5 specific cases where Clartha surfaced a material signal before it appeared in mainstream coverage — including a promoter holding shift in a mid-cap industrial that preceded a 12% correction. Our proof file is ready. The product works. We are now ready to open the waitlist.*

### Q2 — Engineer the First 500
*Months 4–6 | Objective: First paying cohort + public presence*

**What To Do**
- Open the waitlist. Seed it with closed-100 users sharing specific proof points — insight cards, early signals caught, thesis examples built with Clartha.
- Founders go fully public on Twitter/X and LinkedIn. Weekly threads anchored to the three constants. Every post ends with the Clartha proof point. Personal voice only — no brand account yet.
- Product Hunt launch — only after 50 genuine reviews are ready from the closed 100. Do not launch cold.
- Offer closed-100 users founder pricing: Rs. 999/month locked for life if they convert before public launch.
- Launch the daily brief — a personalised morning intelligence summary tied to each user's portfolio. Even a simple version creates the daily habit trigger that defines retention.

**Success Criteria — End of Q2**

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Waitlist sign-ups | 500+ |
| Paying users | 100–200 |
| Day-30 retention (paid cohort 1) | > 65% |
| Founder Twitter/LinkedIn posts | 24+ (3/week) |
| Product Hunt ranking | Top 5 of the day |

**Investor Update — Q2 — Months 4–6**

> *Clartha is live with 180 paying subscribers at Rs. 1,499/month, generating Rs. 2.7L in monthly recurring revenue. Our waitlist stands at 620. Day-30 retention on the first paid cohort is 71%. The daily brief is live and opening rates are tracking at 58% — significantly above industry benchmarks for financial newsletters. Founder content has generated 3,400 organic followers across platforms with zero paid distribution. The product is converting attention to habit.*

### Q3 — Prove Retention
*Months 7–9 | Objective: Retention audit + community layer*

**What To Do**
- Month 7 is a full retention audit. Of the first 200 paying users — how many are still active at day 30, 60, 90? If day-90 retention is above 65%, scale. If below 50%, stop acquiring and fix the product first.
- Launch the community layer — a private Discord or Telegram for Clartha Pro users only. Serious investors talking to serious investors, with Clartha intelligence as shared context. This is where network effects begin.
- Build the referral mechanic: every Clartha insight is shareable as a branded card with the user's handle. When a user shares an early signal and it plays out, that story spreads.
- Introduce the track record feature — users can see how many signals they acted on and what the outcomes were. This is the stickiest feature Clartha can build.
- Begin conversations with Zerodha, Groww, or INDmoney for a distribution partnership.

**Success Criteria — End of Q3**

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Paying users | 500–800 |
| Day-90 retention (all cohorts) | > 65% |
| Community members (Discord/Telegram) | 300+ |
| Referral-sourced sign-ups | > 20% of new users |
| Broker partnership conversations initiated | 2+ |

**Investor Update — Q3 — Months 7–9**

> *Clartha has 680 paying subscribers and Rs. 10.2L in monthly recurring revenue. Day-90 retention across all cohorts is tracking at 67% — above our target threshold. Our private community has 340 members and has become the most active serious investor discussion forum we are aware of in India. The referral engine is working: 24% of new sign-ups in the last 30 days came from existing users sharing Clartha insight cards. We have initiated partnership conversations with two major Indian brokers. The retention story is proven. We are ready to scale.*

### Q4 — Scale What Is Proven
*Months 10–12 | Objective: 2,000–3,000 paying users + Phase 2 foundation*

**What To Do**
- Launch annual plans aggressively. Convert monthly to annual at a 2-month discount (effectively 17% off). This locks in LTV, reduces churn mathematically, and improves cash predictability.
- Push referral and community virality hard. Launch a structured referral programme: 1 month free for every successful referral.
- Execute one high-quality PR moment — not a product story, a category story. Pitch Economic Times Markets, Mint, or Moneycontrol: 'Why serious retail investors are moving to AI-native research.' Clartha is the proof point inside the story.
- Announce or activate one broker/platform distribution partnership.
- Begin Phase 2 foundation: cross-border investor capabilities, NRI market. Build quietly. Launch in Year 2.
- Start the Series A preparation: retention curve, revenue chart, cohort data, NPS.

**Success Criteria — End of Q4**

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Paying users | 2,000 – 3,000 |
| Monthly Recurring Revenue | Rs. 30L – 45L |
| Day-90 retention | > 65% |
| Daily active rate (paid) | > 40% |
| Net Promoter Score | > 50 |
| Annual plan conversion | > 30% of paid base |

**Investor Update — Q4 — Months 10–12**

> *Clartha closes its first year with 2,400 paying subscribers and Rs. 36L in monthly recurring revenue. Day-90 retention is 69%. Daily active rate among paid users is 44%. Net Promoter Score is 58. We have one active broker distribution partnership live, contributing 18% of new user acquisition. Annual plan adoption is at 34% of the paid base, significantly extending LTV. A feature story in Mint covered the emergence of AI-native investment research as a category — Clartha was cited as the leading product in India. We are now raising a Series A to fund Phase 2: multi-market intelligence and the global cross-border investor.*
