# Worked Example: Loomio (Product-Led, Consumer Prosumer)

Loomio is a hypothetical AI-native writing assistant for individual writers — Gen-Z and Millennial, self-serve, daily use. Consumer prosumer PLG.

Use this as a quality, format, and tone reference for **product-led, consumer-facing** GTM strategies. Contrast with `example-clartha.md` (story-led, trust-heavy fintech) to see how the same framework adapts.

---

## Founder Inputs & Research Summary

Loomio is an AI writing assistant for individual writers — journalists, content creators, students, solo professionals. Solo user, daily use, self-serve at $12/mo Pro. Research: category is crowded (Jasper, Copy.ai, Notion AI, ChatGPT). Reviews show churn driver is "feels generic"; individual writers want tools that adapt to their voice, not corporate brand voice. Top TikTok/Reels hashtag #AIwriting has 2.1B views — creators publicly share prompts. Opening: tool built for voice, not business writing.

## Part I — The Constants

### 1 — Sound Like Myself

Every writer has a voice. Generic AI flattens it. The permanent truth: writers who sound like themselves earn trust, get read, and build careers. Writers who sound like a corporate press release are replaceable. This has been true before AI and will be true after.

### 2 — Write Faster Without Writing Worse

Every writer, forever, has wanted to produce more without sacrificing quality. The constraint is attention, not fingers. Any tool that collapses time-to-finished without collapsing quality has permanent demand — regardless of what the tool is powered by.

### 3 — Keep What I Write

Writers own their work. Their drafts, their voice patterns, their style. Any tool that takes ownership — by training on their work, locking their output, or making them dependent on the vendor — eventually loses. This has been true of every writing tool in history, from quills to WordStar to Google Docs.

These three constants are Loomio's North Star. Does this feature, partnership, or decision serve "sound like myself," "write faster without writing worse," or "keep what I write"? If not, deprioritise.

## Part III — GTM Strategy

### Category

Not "AI writing tool" — there are 200 of those. **Voice-native writing.** The tool that adapts to your voice, not corporate brand voice. Every Loomio output reflects the specific writer who wrote it; generic AI output is the category failure mode.

### Activation Ladder (PLG equivalent of Trust Ladder)

| Stage | What You Are Proving | To Whom |
|-------|---------------------|---------|
| 0 → 1 | The first draft feels like the writer's voice | Internal + 20 beta writers |
| 1 → 500 | Writers form a daily habit + share outputs publicly | Early creator community |
| 500 → 5,000 | Free-to-paid works; referral compounds | Active writer network |
| 5,000 → 50,000 | Creator culture adopts Loomio as default | Mass prosumer market |

### Positioning Statement

> *Loomio gives individual writers an AI that adapts to their voice — not a corporate brand voice — without sacrificing speed. One tool for sounding like yourself, writing faster, and keeping what you write.*

### Product-Led Engine

- **Activation hook:** 10-minute onboarding that trains a voice model from 3 samples of the writer's past work. First output reads like them — that's the aha moment.
- **Share mechanic:** Every Loomio draft has a branded share link. Writers share the finished piece + "written with my Loomio voice" tag.
- **Template / SEO motion:** Public gallery of voice profiles by genre (journalist, essayist, copywriter, student). Each is a landing page.
- **Referral:** 1 month free Pro for each friend who activates (completes first draft).

### Creative-Led Layer (supporting)

Loomio's public voice is that of a defiant, taste-forward writer — not a SaaS brand. Distinctive visual identity; packaging / UI with opinion. Founders publish on TikTok / Reels showing "here's the generic ChatGPT output vs. here's my Loomio output" — the before/after IS the marketing.

### Channel-Led Layer (supporting)

TikTok + Reels as the dominant channel. Not paid at first — organic creator partnerships with 10–50 writers who already have audiences and let Loomio power their writing visibly. Each creator's output IS the ad.

### Pricing Architecture

| Tier | Price | Target | Key Features |
|------|-------|--------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | New writers / trial | 5 drafts/month, one voice profile, share links |
| Pro | $12/month | Active writer | Unlimited drafts, voice tuning, export to Substack/Medium/Docs, priority generation |
| Creator | $29/month | Publishers / creators | Multi-voice profiles, team mode, analytics on what converts readers |

Annual at 20% discount — only offered after Day-30 activation. Don't launch with a discount; launch with a waitlist. First 500 users get lifetime founder pricing ($8/month locked) — this is the marketing.

## Part IV — Year 1 Execution Plan

### Q1 — Narrow the ICP
*Months 1–3 | Objective: Find the 500 writers who love this the most.*

**What To Do**
- Choose a specific writer archetype (early hypothesis: independent journalists + essayists). Invite 200 hand-picked via Twitter/X, Substack, journalism Slacks. Full Pro access free.
- Ship the voice training flow. Iterate weekly on the onboarding — the only metric is: did first draft land on voice?
- Founders publish on TikTok / Reels daily. Format: "I made ChatGPT and Loomio both write this. Watch how different they sound." One-minute videos. Low production.
- Private Slack community for the 200 early writers. Founders in every thread.
- Track one metric only: Day-7 return rate of new cohorts. This is PMF signal.

**Success Criteria — End of Q1**

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Early writers onboarded | 200 |
| Day-7 return rate | > 50% |
| TikTok/Reels posts | 60+ |
| Published pieces credited to Loomio | 30+ |

> *We onboarded 220 hand-picked writers from journalism and essay communities. Day-7 return rate is 56%. The voice-training onboarding lands — 71% of users said the first draft "sounded like them" in the in-app survey. We seeded a private community where writers share tuned voice profiles; 40+ have swapped profiles already. Our TikTok account posting side-by-side generic-vs-Loomio comparisons has generated 2.1M organic views and surfaced 30 writers who independently reached out to try it. ## Part V — Objection Handling

### Objection: "You're just a wrapper on GPT."

**Full:** The model is a commodity. What we own is the writer's voice profile — a proprietary dataset built from each writer's prior work — and the drafting workflow embedded into every writer's daily tool chain. A wrapper is a chat box over an API. We're the only AI writing tool where the writer's own corpus trains the model and stays theirs. Our share link appears at the bottom of pieces in major publications; no wrapper gets cited by name in The Atlantic.

**One-liner:** We're not wrapping GPT. We're the voice layer on top of it.

---

### Objection: "Jasper and Copy.ai already exist."

**Full:** Jasper and Copy.ai built for business marketers writing corporate copy. Our user is the individual writer — journalists, essayists, students. The feature overlap is surface; the ICP is opposite. Our Day-30 retention with that ICP is 51%; Jasper's publicly reported figure in their 2023 filing was sub-30% for non-enterprise. We're not in the same category — we just happen to share a technology.

**One-liner:** They're building for brands. We're building for writers.

---

### Objection: "This will commoditize. GPT-5 will do this natively."

**Full:** GPT improvements are our best friend. We don't differentiate on model quality — we differentiate on the voice profile (proprietary), the drafting workflow (embedded), and the writer community (moat). Every model improvement makes our product better at no cost to us. The moat was never the model; it's the voice, the workflow, and the fact that 4,100 writers have a year of their own work trained into Loomio that doesn't transfer elsewhere.

**One-liner:** GPT-5 doesn't threaten us. It upgrades us.
